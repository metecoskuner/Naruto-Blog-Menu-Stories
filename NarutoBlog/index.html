NARUTO is a manga series created by Masashi Kishimoto that was serialized in Weekly Shonen Jump. Met with immediate popularity following its initial publication in 1999, the series went on to receive an anime adaptation that commenced broadcasting in 2002. The manga concluded in 2014 with a total of 700 chapters. The total number of print copies sold globally exceeds 250 million, and even now NARUTO events, games, merchandise, and more continue to be created and released for fans to enjoy—a testament to the series’ enduring influence and popularity around the world. BORUTO: Naruto Next Generations (Supervisor: Masashi Kishimoto / Art: Mikio Ikemoto), the sequel to the NARUTO series, is currently being published in V Jump under the creative supervision of Masashi Kishimoto. Dive into the world of NARUTO and experience this amazing story for yourself!

Naruto Uzumaki is the main protagonist of the Naruto series. He is a young ninja who dreams of becoming the Hokage, the leader of his village, to gain recognition and respect. Despite his troubled past and the challenges he faces, Naruto's determination, resilience, and unwavering spirit make him a beloved character. His journey is one of growth, friendship, and self-discovery.

The World of Chakra

Chakra is the cornerstone of all jutsu in the Naruto universe, representing a form of spiritual and physical energy that ninja manipulate to perform their abilities. This mystical energy is a blend of the body’s physical energy and the mind’s spiritual energy. Every ninja must master the control and application of chakra to execute their techniques effectively. The chakra system is fundamental to the understanding of how ninjas operate within their world.

  • Basic Chakra: The base chakra is composed of physical and spiritual energy. Physical energy is derived from the body's vitality and stamina, while spiritual energy comes from mental focus and training. The combination of these energies results in chakra, which is used for various jutsu.
  • Chakra Nature Transformations: Ninja can manipulate chakra to align with different elemental natures: Fire, Water, Earth, Wind, and Lightning. Each ninja usually has a natural affinity for one or more of these elements, influencing their preferred combat style and techniques.
  • Advanced Chakra Techniques: Advanced techniques include Sage Mode and Tailed Beast transformations, which require unique methods of chakra control and utilization. Sage Mode, for instance, involves harmonizing one's chakra with nature energy, while Tailed Beasts grant massive amounts of chakra to their hosts, or Jinchūriki.

Hidden Villages and Their Significance

The Naruto universe is divided into several hidden villages, each serving as the primary setting for the series. These villages are not just geographical locations but also cultural and political entities, each with its own unique characteristics and significance. The leaders of these villages, known as Kage, play crucial roles in maintaining their villages' stability and security.

  • Konohagakure (Leaf Village): Known as the strongest and most influential of the Hidden Villages, Konohagakure is situated in the Land of Fire. It has produced some of the most powerful ninjas, including Naruto Uzumaki and Sasuke Uchiha. The village is led by the Hokage, who oversees the protection and governance of the village.
  • Sunagakure (Sand Village): Located in the Land of Wind, Sunagakure is known for its harsh desert environment and its formidable Sand Ninjas. The village is ruled by the Kazekage, and Gaara, who initially appears as an antagonist, becomes a prominent leader in the series.
  • Kirigakure (Mist Village): Situated in the Land of Water, Kirigakure was infamous for its brutal training methods and was once known as the "Village of the Bloody Mist." However, under the leadership of the Fifth Mizukage, Mei Terumi, the village undergoes significant reforms.
  • Kumogakure (Cloud Village): This village, in the Land of Lightning, is renowned for its powerful ninjas and advanced combat techniques. The Raikage, the village leader, is known for their exceptional strength and speed, with the Fourth Raikage, A, being particularly notable.
  • Iwagakure (Stone Village): Located in the Land of Earth, Iwagakure is known for its strategic and earth-based combat techniques. The village is led by the Tsuchikage, and its leaders, like Ōnoki, are renowned for their experience and powerful abilities.

The Tailed Beasts (Bijū)

Tailed Beasts, or Bijū, are powerful creatures that possess immense chakra and are integral to the Naruto universe. Each Bijū has its own unique abilities and characteristics, making them both revered and feared. Historically, these beasts were either sealed within humans, known as Jinchūriki, or were hunted by various factions for their power.

  • Kurama (Nine-Tailed Fox): The most powerful of the Bijū, Kurama, also known as the Nine-Tailed Fox, possesses immense chakra and destructive power. Naruto Uzumaki is the current Jinchūriki of Kurama, and their relationship evolves from animosity to a strong bond of cooperation.
  • Shukaku (One-Tailed Tanuki): Known for its ability to control sand, Shukaku is a formidable Bijū sealed within Gaara of the Sand Village. Its abilities make it a significant asset in both offense and defense during battles.
  • Gyūki (Eight-Tailed Ox): Gyūki is known for its immense strength and its ability to generate powerful chakra-based attacks. It is sealed within Killer Bee, a skilled ninja from the Cloud Village, who manages to harness its power effectively.
  • Rōshi (Four-Tailed Monkey): Rōshi is a Bijū with the ability to produce and manipulate lava. Its former Jinchūriki, Rōshi, was known for his strong will and combat prowess.
  • Son Gokū (Four-Tailed Beast): Also known as the Four-Tailed Beast, Son Gokū is known for its control over magma and volcanic techniques. It is an ancient and powerful creature with a deep connection to its previous hosts.
  • Yūbi (Eight-Tailed Beast): Yūbi is known for its control over dark energy and its massive size. It is a significant player in the dynamics of the Bijū and their influence over the ninja world.
  • Saiken (Six-Tailed Beast): Saiken, the Six-Tailed Beast, has the ability to produce and control corrosive substances. Its role in the series is crucial due to its unique abilities and its impact on its Jinchūriki.
  • Chomei (Seven-Tailed Beetle): Chomei is a Bijū with the ability to generate powerful gusts and control insect-like energy. Its role and abilities make it a distinctive and formidable creature in the Naruto universe.

Major Organizations and Their Roles

Throughout the Naruto series, various organizations play critical roles in shaping the world and its conflicts. These groups range from criminal syndicates to peacekeeping forces, each with distinct goals and methods.

  • Akatsuki: A notorious organization composed of rogue ninjas from various villages, Akatsuki seeks to capture all Tailed Beasts to achieve their goal of world domination and bring about a new era of peace through control. Led by Nagato (Pain) and later by Shin Uchiha, Akatsuki is a major antagonistic force in the series.
  • Konoha 11: This group consists of the main characters from Konohagakure who initially form a team during their early years as ninjas. Members like Naruto, Sasuke, Sakura, and others represent the core heroes of the series, each contributing to the defense and growth of their village.
  • Root: An underground organization within Konohagakure, Root operates under the leadership of Danzo Shimura. Known for its secrecy and extreme measures, Root’s primary goal is to protect the village and its interests, sometimes at the cost of ethics and personal freedoms.
  • The Seven Ninja Swordsmen of the Mist: A legendary group from Kirigakure, known for wielding powerful swords with unique abilities. The group has a significant impact on the series, especially during the Fourth Shinobi World War.
  • The Twelve Guardian Ninja: Elite ninjas tasked with protecting the Land of Water’s royal family. Their role is crucial in the political and military structure of their respective countries, offering both protection and strategic advantage.
